    Assignment 2A: Design Inspiration Poster

    The Poster:

    Tasked to conceptualise a website design with the use of publicly available data APIs, I chose to make light work on designing a one-stop portal for all things food related.

    Known as Food and Business Brisbane, FBB, it provides informative services and online application and reporting services to both consumers and providers of food respectively.

    The inspiration for this website came from my difficulty understanding the nuances of the food safety and consumer laws dictated by the Queensland Government and Brisbane City Council. Additionally, as an international student, it provides information on where to find good eats in Brisbane.

    In an attempt to address the above issues, I intended to create a website with its central focus on informing non-locals of upcoming food events and weekly markets around the city, while allowing food business owners to easily apply or renew their food business licenses and for consumers to make a food safety violation report easily.

    By performing an environmental scan, the following are similar existing applications in my domain:

  • Brisbane Food Trucks
  • QLD Govt: Make a food safety complaint online
  • BCC Food business licence info page

    • The user interface would be similar to Brisbane City Council's webpage.

    Planning Process

    Our vision for okDoggy:

    okDoggy is a convenient and intuitive online map for dog owners to search and locate nearby pet focused services in Brisbane, such as groomers, pet hospitals, veterinarians, etc.

    Purpose:

    okDoggy is a convenient and intuitive online map for dog owners to search and locate nearby pet focused services in Brisbane, such as groomers, pet hospitals, veterinarians, etc.

    okDoggy intends to optimise, simplify and replace the methods by which dog owners/lovers find dog parks.

    okDoggy creates a swift, efficient and straightforward portal to an array of dog park information. Through intuitive navigation services, users are not just finding parks but discovering communities where they can share unique moments and daily experiences with their beloved pets.

    The background inspiration for this website stems from dog owners who require many apps for sharing their photos and acquiring resources and information for their dogs.

    Target Audience:

    Our primary target audience are dog owners. This includes those who are enthusiastic about discovering new dog parks and sharing their experiences with a like-minded community. okDoggy allows individuals to find the best recreational spaces for their pet dog.

    Our secondary target audience is people looking to get a dog, allowing them easy access to a map of all dog parks, a list of user posted images and easing their way into the world of owning a dog.

    Design Process

    For this assignment, we worked on sketching out our low fidelity prototype by free hand drawing what we visualise our website to be out on a tablet, then building our high fidelity prototype on Figma. Having prior knowledge and experience with the application, I taught my teammates tips and tricks on creating a high-fi prototype efficiently.

    The design inspiration for our website stems from dog owners who require many apps to:
    (1) be involved in a tight knitted community of dog owners, and
    (2) access reliable and valuable resources and information for their dogs.

    It utilises the Brisbane City Council's dog off-leash dog areas data API (resource_id: f65b95bc-3c87-4aa7-822a-9eec35cd3ae6) to retrieve information of dog parks, as well as the Google Maps Javascript API for building dynamic and interactive maps to display nearby places of interests.

    • Critical Reflection for MVP

    Weeks before the MVP presentation, I told my team I was not confident in implementing a blog page in time for the MVP presentation. As the MVP presentation intended for us to present our most valuable feature of our website, I focused on getting the map functioning. Hence, in favour of delivering the promised MVP product, we scrapped the idea for having a blog page for added user interactivity. (or so I thought)

    My Personal Reflection

    Many tensions arose within our group after this sprint. One of my teammate decided by himself we needed a blog page. He was insistent on it having implemented. His intentions and ideas for it are not very well-founded. I advised that it was unrealistic in the delivery time considering the number of functionalities we wanted versus the limited time resource and knowledge of all parties. He decided however, on his own accord, to work on a blog page and utilise his website design from one of his assignments in DECO1400.

    The MVP product and functionalities presented has been 100% my doing, while my other teammates worked on the slides.

    Ying, who was usually on top of things, had some minor disagreements when working with our other teammate. He had insisted that certain criteria or points to address in the presentation were not needed. While my other teammate and I had been in the past studio sessions where we had been previously instructed numerous times that the presentation should focus on these few points, these same points, in the end were left out of the presentation by the discretion of our other teammate. Being left out in the making of the presentation, it is my biggest regret I did not oversee the quality and maintained the high standard I had envisioned for this project and myself.

    At the end of the day, I provided unparalleled utility to my team, and the outcome presented would not have had nearly the same value had I not been involved.
